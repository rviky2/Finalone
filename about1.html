The Divine Majesty of Lord Srinivasa

The idol of Lord Srinivasa is one of the most sacred and powerful murtis (divine images) in Hinduism. Every feature, including its posture, ornaments, and divine marks, carries deep spiritual symbolism.

1. General Features of the Idol

Material: Krishna Shila (black stone), known for retaining divine energy.

Height: Approximately 8 feet tall.

Posture: Upright in Samabhanga (balanced posture), signifying equilibrium.

Expression: A calm yet majestic face, radiating divine compassion.

3. Head & Crown

Shankha Mukuta (Crown): A tall golden crown studded with precious gems, signifying supreme authority.

Tilak (Namam) on the Forehead: A white U-shaped tilak with a red/yellow streak, symbolizing Vishnu and Lakshmi.

4. Ornaments & Sacred Decorations

Sri Lakshmi Srinivasa Dollar: A golden ornament on the chest symbolizing the presence of Goddess Lakshmi.

Nagabharanam: Golden snake ornaments representing Ananta Shesha and divine protection.

Vaijayanti Mala: A sacred garland with 108 beads, symbolizing devotion and cosmic energy.
