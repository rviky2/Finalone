Discover the Divine at Vaikunta Campus

The idol of Lord Srinivasa enshrined in the temple at Vaikunta Campus, located within the serene premises of Srinivas Institute of Technology, Mangaluru, stands as a symbol of unwavering devotion and divine grace. Crafted with exquisite artistry and deep spiritual significance, the idol radiates an aura of peace and sanctity, drawing devotees into a realm of tranquility and devotion. The temple serves as a spiritual sanctuary, where prayers, rituals, and divine chants create an atmosphere of sacred reverence. Devotees from all walks of life visit this holy site to seek blessings, offer their prayers, and experience a profound connection with the divine. The temple architecture, adorned with intricate carvings and traditional designs, reflects the timeless essence of spirituality and faith. Nestled amidst lush greenery and a peaceful environment, Vaikunta Campus offers a retreat for the soul, fostering a sense of inner peace and spiritual upliftment. Whether during auspicious festivals, daily rituals, or moments of personal contemplation, the presence of Lord Srinivasa at Vaikunta Campus continues to inspire and bless all who step into this divine abode.

The temple is not only a place of worship but also a center of cultural and spiritual gatherings, where devotees come together in unity and devotion. Special poojas, bhajans, and religious discourses further enhance the spiritual vibrancy of this sacred space. With its divine ambiance and tranquil surroundings, Vaikunta Campus remains a beacon of faith, guiding countless souls on their spiritual journey.

Srinivasa Kalyanotsava
February 22, 2024

+

By sitmng

On the auspicious day of February 22, 2024, the divine celebrations at Srinivasa Institute of Technology Valachil Mangalore unfolded with various sacred rituals beginning at 5 AM. The morning commenced with the Gana Homa, Prathista Prasanna Pooja, Kalashaabisheka, Vishnu Sahasranama Havana, Kalpaka Pooja, and Dampati Pooja, each performed with precision and devotion. The Mantrakshette ceremony further enriched the spiritual atmosphere. Under the holy guidance of Dr. Srinivasa Rao Ji, the Kalasha Pratistapana took place, consecrating the sacred Kalasha with divine significance. Following this, Lord Srinivasa's idol was consecrated, signifying the divine presence on the premises.
